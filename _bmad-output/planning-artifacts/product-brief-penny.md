# Product Brief: Penny

## Executive Summary

Penny is a free, web-first financial companion for teenagers — a complete rebrand and redesign of PiggyMetrics built around a single insight: teens don't need a budgeting app, they need a saving buddy. At the heart of Penny is an animated pig mascot (also named Penny) who acts as a proactive financial advisor, emotional anchor, and habit coach. Every interaction flows through her.

The teen personal finance market is large ($10.27B in 2025, growing at 8.2% CAGR) and actively underserved. Copper — the closest thing to a teen-first, goal-oriented finance app — shut down in May 2024, leaving over 1 million users without a replacement. Greenlight and GoHenry feel like parental surveillance tools. Step requires a bank card. No competitor offers what Penny does: a no-bank-required, goal-first, web-first experience that treats teens as capable adults.

Penny launches as a fully functional PWA (Progressive Web App) — all existing PiggyMetrics features rebuilt with a teen-native UI — and is completely free. No bank connection, no parental approval, no subscription. Just a teen, a goal, and Penny.

## The Problem

Teens want to save money but existing tools fail them in three ways:

1. **They require a bank account or debit card.** Most teens don't have one, and getting one requires parental involvement — a friction point that kills adoption before it starts.
2. **They feel like surveillance, not empowerment.** Greenlight and GoHenry are designed for parents first. Teens experience them as monitoring tools, not personal finance tools.
3. **They speak adult.** "Income," "expense," "budget" — the language of existing apps signals that this product wasn't made for you. Teens disengage before they even understand what the app does.

The result: 64% of teens are motivated to save (RBC survey), but the tools available either exclude them entirely or treat them like children. Financial anxiety is endemic in Gen Z — over 50% live paycheck to paycheck despite $450B+ in collective spending power. The habit formation window is open. The tools aren't there.

## The Solution

Penny is a PWA where teens set a saving goal, meet Penny, and start tracking their money — in under 2 minutes, with no bank account required.

**The core experience:**
- **Goal-first onboarding:** "What are you saving for?" is the first question. Enter the item, the price, the date. Penny calculates the weekly saving target instantly.
- **Penny as companion:** Penny's mood reflects financial health. She celebrates wins, gently nudges on streaks, and delivers a weekly honest recap. Her voice is "supportive older sibling" — never preachy, never corporate.
- **Teen-native UI:** Dark mode default, stories-style weekly summaries, emoji spending breakdowns, shareable milestone cards. The language is "Money In / Money Out," not "Income / Expense."
- **The What If Simulator:** Slider-based: "Cut bubble tea in half → save $36 extra → AirPods 3 weeks sooner." Output is a shareable card — designed to be screenshot-worthy and spread organically on TikTok and Instagram.
- **Habit mechanics:** Daily logging streaks, Penny's Weekly Roast (opt-in), shareable Saver Level cards.

Penny's structural moat is the no-bank-required model. Copper's collapse proved that bank-dependent teen fintech is fragile. Penny sidesteps this entirely — and in doing so, removes the single biggest adoption barrier for teens.

The PWA-first approach is a secondary moat: no App Store friction, no parental approval required for download, shareable via link, and installable on the home screen. Teens discover Penny through a TikTok link and are using it 90 seconds later.

## Who This Serves

**Primary: Teens aged 13–19** who want to save for something specific — AirPods, sneakers, a trip, a car — and want a tool that feels like it was made for them. They have some income (allowance, part-time job, birthday money) and a goal in mind. They're already motivated; they just need a system that doesn't feel like homework.

Penny is teen-only by design. No parental dashboard, no oversight mode, no family features in v1. This is a deliberate positioning choice: Penny is the first teen finance tool that trusts teens.

## Success Criteria

**At 6 months post-launch:**
- Fully functional PWA with all PiggyMetrics features rebuilt in teen-native UI
- **Activation:** 40%+ of registered users complete goal setup in onboarding
- **Habit formation:** 30%+ of active users maintain a 7-day logging streak
- **Feature adoption:** What If Simulator used by 50%+ of active users within first week
- **Outcome signal:** 25%+ of users who set a goal log at least one "Money In" entry within 7 days
- **Qualitative:** Teens describe Penny as "actually made for me" in user feedback

## Scope

**In for v1:**
- Full PWA: all existing PiggyMetrics functionality (accounts, statistics, notifications) rebuilt with teen-native UI — mobile-responsive, installable, offline-capable
- Penny mascot system: mood states, voice guide, onboarding introduction
- Goal-first onboarding flow
- Teen language overhaul ("Money In / Money Out," Penny-voiced navigation)
- Dark mode default
- Stories-style weekly summary
- Daily logging streak
- What If Simulator with shareable output card
- Shareable milestone cards
- Social login (Google/Apple)

**Explicitly out for v1:**
- Bank/card integration
- Parental dashboard or oversight
- Friend challenges / social features
- Penny Wrapped annual recap
- Native mobile app (PWA covers mobile experience)
- Family wishlist / gifting
- Monetization layer

**Open question for legal review before build:**
- Age verification and consent flow for 13–15 year olds (COPPA 2025 compliance)

## Vision

If Penny succeeds, it becomes the default financial companion for an entire generation — the app teens recommend to each other, the one that shows up in TikTok "how I saved for X" videos, the one that gets credited when a 19-year-old buys their first car with cash.

In 2-3 years: social saving challenges, Penny Wrapped, a native mobile app, school/classroom partnerships (26 US states now require personal finance education — Penny is the only no-bank tool that works in a classroom), and potentially a pathway to real banking features — built on a foundation of trust that no bank-first competitor can replicate.
